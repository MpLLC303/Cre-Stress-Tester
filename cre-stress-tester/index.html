§ 01 — What it does

A second opinion on your underwriting, before you walk into committee.

Paste a commercial real estate deal memo or pro forma summary, and get the structured critique an experienced acquisitions associate would produce — red flags named by pattern, fragility ranked by dollar impact, and a specific verification checklist of questions to bring back to the sponsor.

The tool is deliberately skeptical by default. It refuses to validate a deal because the user wants validation, refuses to fabricate live market data it doesn't have, and refuses to flag reasonable deals just to seem thorough.

§ 02 — How it works

Four steps from paste to pressure-tested.

Step 01
Paste the Memo
Deal memo body, pro forma summary, or key underwriting assumptions. Optional intake fields let you specify asset class, market, strategy, hold, and target IRR.
Step 02
Grounded Retrieval
The tool pulls from six curated knowledge files — a 25-entry red flag catalog, asset-class market ranges, a sensitivity framework, and a cap rate cycle reference.
Step 03
Rule-Bound Critique
Seven non-negotiable rules govern the output: no fabricated numbers, cite the red flag by name, quantify fragility, respect the cycle, default to skepticism, no over-flagging, end with the disclaimer.
Step 04
Structured Output
Deal snapshot, named red flags, ranked fragility assessment, a verification checklist with sources, and an honest list of what the tool could not assess.

Live output · Persona A · value-add multifamily Real Run
§01 · Deal Snapshot

284-unit Class B garden-style multifamily (1986 vintage) in Phoenix West Valley. Purchase price $65.3M ($230K/unit), 5.25% going-in cap. Value-add strategy with $8K/unit interior renovations and stated 18.2% IRR target.

§02 · Red Flags Identified
Unrealistic Rent Growth (KF-02 #1) 5.75% sustained annual rent growth over five years is ~200 bps above long-run submarket averages. A reversion to 3.5% growth compresses Year-5 NOI by roughly 10–12%.
Exit Cap Compression (KF-02 #12) 50 bps compression from 5.25% entry to 4.75% exit assumes a tighter rate environment at sale than at acquisition. A flat exit cap of 5.25% reduces IRR from 18.2% to approximately 13–14%.
Interest-Only Masking Amortization Cliff (KF-02 #19) Full-term IO for the entire 5-year hold defers all principal repayment to refinance or sale. Stress-test year-1 cash-on-cash on 30-year amortization and refinance risk at current market rates plus 100 bps.
§03 · Verification Checklist (excerpt)
  • Pull trailing 10-year submarket rent growth from CoStar; compare to the 5.75% projection.
  • Check exit cap against trailing 6-month Class B multifamily trades in the submarket per CBRE Research.
  • Confirm Maricopa County reassessment-on-sale policy; re-size year-1 tax bill against purchase price, not seller's T-12.

§ 05 — What this tool does not do

The refusals are the point.

A stress tester that tells you what you want to hear isn't a stress tester. The tool is built with explicit refusal anchors for the failure modes that break similar products:

Non-negotiable refusals
  • Does not make buy, sell, or hold recommendations.
  • Does not predict future cap rates, interest rates, or market direction.
  • Does not fabricate live market data — if a current cap rate, loan spread, or rent comp is unknown, it says so and directs to CBRE, Green Street, CoStar, or NAREIT.
  • Does not validate a deal because the user frames it favorably.
  • Does not invent flags to seem thorough — defensible deals get called defensible.

Iteration path

Three versions of the system prompt were built and compared: v1 (role priming only, no knowledge files), v2 (knowledge files integrated, structured intake), and v3 (seven numbered rules, mandatory XML output schema, refusal anchors, disclaimer). v3 combines seven of the ten engineering techniques in a single prompt and is the version running in production.

Model selection & cost

Benchmarked Claude Sonnet 4.6 against Claude Haiku 4.5 on a four-persona test set (value-add multifamily, gateway core office, industrial control case, hospitality reflag). Prompt caching reduces per-call input cost by ~90% after the first call, since the 10K-token knowledge base is static across calls.

Knowledge base

Six curated files totaling ~10,000 tokens: a CRE underwriting fundamentals reference, a 25-entry red flag catalog organized into five categories (revenue, expense, cap rate & exit, debt & capital structure, structural & market), asset-class market ranges, a sensitivity analysis framework, sample annotated memos for pattern teaching, and a cap rate cycle reference. Source material drawn from CBRE Research, NAREIT, NCREIF, Green Street Advisors, NAIOP, and Adventures in CRE.

Testing & evaluation

Four test personas with embedded red-flag answer keys (seven flags, six flags, four flags, six flags respectively). Persona C is a control case designed specifically to test for over-flagging — a failure mode as severe as missing real flags. The tool is scored on five dimensions: red flag detection, citation discipline, fragility analysis, no-hallucination compliance, and analytical depth.
