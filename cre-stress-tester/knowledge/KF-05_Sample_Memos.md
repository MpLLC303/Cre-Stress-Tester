# Sample Annotated Deal Memos

Teaching examples of defensible vs. weak CRE underwriting. The weak memo's red flags are cited by name from KF-02.

---

## Memo 1 — Defensible Underwriting (Class B Industrial, Phoenix MSA)

**Deal Summary**
Acquisition of Desert Ridge Logistics Center, a 285,000 sf Class B industrial distribution facility built in 2007, located in the Sky Harbor submarket of Phoenix. The asset is 94% leased to five tenants with a weighted average lease term of 4.1 years. Clear height is 30', with 42 dock-high doors and 2.2/1,000 sf parking. Purchase price is $48,450,000 ($170/sf), reflecting a 6.10% going-in cap rate on in-place NOI of $2,955,000.

**Key Assumptions**
- Entry cap rate: 6.10% (supported by four Q3–Q4 trades in the Sky Harbor/Airport submarket ranging 5.95%–6.35% per RCA)
- Rent growth: 3.5% annual years 1–5, moderating from current submarket trailing-24-month growth of 4.2% per CoStar
- Exit cap rate: 6.25% (going-in + 15 bps asset-aging adjustment)
- Opex growth: 3.25% annual
- Debt: 60% LTV, 5.85% fixed rate, 5-year term, 2 years interest-only, sized per current Life Co term sheet dated 30 days ago
- Hold period: 5 years
- Target IRR / Multiple: 12.8% / 1.72x

**Thesis**
Phoenix industrial fundamentals remain above long-run averages, with submarket vacancy at 7.8% against a 10-year average of 9.1%. The subject's location within 4 miles of Sky Harbor and the Loop 202 interchange supports last-mile logistics demand. Two of five in-place tenants are 15%+ below market on expiring leases in years 2 and 3, providing mark-to-market upside without speculative lease-up risk. Construction pipeline in the submarket is 3.1% of inventory, below the MSA average of 4.8%. Sponsor is contributing 8% co-invest of equity with a 20% promote over a 9% pref.

**Why this memo is defensible:**

- Entry cap is sourced to specific recent trades, not anchored to "market" claims — addresses the discipline required by KF-02 #14 (Cap Rate Without Source).
- Exit cap is wider than going-in cap by 15 bps, reflecting the standard asset-aging convention and avoiding the compression pattern in KF-02 #12.
- Rent growth (3.5%) is below trailing submarket growth (4.2%) — the underwriting is more conservative than recent data would allow, leaving room for moderation.
- Supply pipeline is explicitly addressed (3.1% of inventory vs. MSA 4.8%), directly countering the pattern in KF-02 #22.
- NOI upside comes from in-place mark-to-market on expiring leases, not from speculative new lease-up or concession burn-off — the upside is quantifiable from the rent roll.
- Sponsor co-invest of 8% is within institutional norms for value-add, addressing the alignment concern in KF-02 #25.

## Memo 2 — Weak Underwriting (Class B Multifamily, Sun Belt secondary)

**Deal Summary**
Acquisition of The Palms at Harbor Bay, a 248-unit Class B multifamily property built in 1986, located in a growing Sun Belt secondary market. The asset is 93% occupied with average in-place rents of $1,485/month. Purchase price is $52,080,000 ($210,000/unit), reflecting a 5.15% going-in cap rate. Business plan includes $7,500/unit interior renovations across 180 classic units.

**Key Assumptions**
- Entry cap rate: 5.15% (in line with market for Class B multifamily in the submarket)
- Rent growth: 5.25% annual years 1–5, reflecting strong submarket fundamentals and in-migration trends
- Post-renovation rent premium: $285/unit/month, supported by comparable rents at The Metropolitan, The Sterling, and Harborview Residences
- Exit cap rate: 4.75% (supported by recent submarket transactions in 2021 trading at 4.25%–4.75%)
- Opex growth: 1.75% annual, with property taxes growing 2.0% from current $625,000 base
- Debt: 75% LTV agency loan, 5.50% fixed rate, 5 years full-term interest-only
- Hold period: 5 years
- Target IRR / Multiple: 19.5% / 2.1x

**Thesis**
The Sun Belt market has demonstrated strong demand for renovated workforce housing, and the subject offers compelling value-add upside through a proven interior renovation program. Absorption of renovated units is projected at 18 units per month, reaching stabilized 96% occupancy within 10 months of renovation completion. Other income grows from $95/unit/month to $165/unit/month by year 3 through expanded RUBS billing, parking fees, and amenity packages. Total capitalization includes $39M senior debt and $13M equity, with remaining structure detailed in the partnership agreement.

**Embedded red flags (cited by name from KF-02):**

- **Flag #14: Cap Rate Without Source** — "Entry cap rate: 5.15% (in line with market for Class B multifamily in the submarket)."
- **Flag #1: Unrealistic Rent Growth** — "Rent growth: 5.25% annual years 1–5, reflecting strong submarket fundamentals and in-migration trends."
- **Flag #2: Cherry-Picked Rent Comparables** — "Post-renovation rent premium: $285/unit/month, supported by comparable rents at The Metropolitan, The Sterling, and Harborview Residences."
- **Flag #15: Cycle-Blind Comp Selection** — "Exit cap rate: 4.75% (supported by recent submarket transactions in 2021 trading at 4.25%–4.75%)."
- **Flag #12: Exit Cap Compression** — Exit cap of 4.75% versus going-in cap of 5.15%.
- **Flag #7: Opex Growth Below Inflation** — "Opex growth: 1.75% annual."
- **Flag #8: Tax Reassessment Ignored** — "property taxes growing 2.0% from current $625,000 base" (ignores reassessment on sale).
- **Flag #18: LTV Above Lender Appetite** — "Debt: 75% LTV agency loan."
- **Flag #19: Interest-Only Masking Amortization Cliff** — "5 years full-term interest-only."
- **Flag #3: Aggressive Lease-Up Pace** — "Absorption of renovated units is projected at 18 units per month, reaching stabilized 96% occupancy within 10 months."
- **Flag #5: Overstated Other Income** — "Other income grows from $95/unit/month to $165/unit/month by year 3."
- **Flag #20: Hidden Mezz or Preferred Equity** — "remaining structure detailed in the partnership agreement."
- **Flag #23: Job Growth and Migration Unaddressed** — "strong submarket fundamentals and in-migration trends" (generic claim without data).

**What an analyst would say in committee:**

- "The entry cap rate has no sourced comp — the 5.15% is either aspirational or cherry-picked. Before we spend another hour on this, pull me the last six months of submarket trades and let's see where this actually prices."
- "The exit cap is tighter than the going-in cap and the supporting comps are from 2021. This deal is priced for a rate environment that no longer exists. Rerun with exit cap at 5.50% and tell me what IRR drops to."
- "Opex growth of 1.75% is below any reasonable inflation assumption, and taxes are being held flat through a sale in a reassessment state. Rerun with 3% opex growth and a year-one tax bill sized to purchase price."
- "75% agency LTV isn't happening on this asset at current lender grids, and five years of full-term IO is masking a refinance cliff we haven't stress-tested. What does IRR look like on 30-year amortization and current quoted proceeds? And I want the full capital stack — what's hiding in 'partnership structure'?"
