### Typical Annual Rent Growth Assumptions (defensible underwriting)
- Conservative: 2.0%–2.5% [VERIFY]
- Market: 2.5%–3.5% [VERIFY]

### Typical Operating Expense Ratios (% of EGI)
- Conservative: 38%–42% [VERIFY]
- Market: 42%–48% [VERIFY]

### Typical Hold Periods by Strategy
- Core: 7–10+ years
- Value-Add: 3–5 years
- Opportunistic: 3–5 years

### Common Red-Flag Thresholds for This Asset Class
- "Unrealistic Rent Growth" (KF-02 #1) — sustained projections above 4% annual on stabilized Class B/C product warrant explicit comp support.
- "Aggressive Lease-Up Pace" (KF-02 #3) — value-add absorption assumptions exceeding 12–15 units/month should be benchmarked against trailing submarket lease-up data [VERIFY].
- "Tax Reassessment Ignored" (KF-02 #8) — recurring failure pattern in CA, FL, TX acquisitions; verify post-sale tax basis on every multifamily deal.
- "Exit Cap Compression" (KF-02 #12) — frequent on Sun Belt value-add memos that anchor exit cap to 2021 trade comps.
- "Replacement Reserves Understated" (KF-02 #10) — pre-1990 vintage assets frequently underwritten below the $300–500/unit lender minimum [VERIFY].

## Office

### Typical Going-In Cap Rate Ranges (institutional quality)
- Class A / Primary markets: 6.50%–8.00% [VERIFY — wide dispersion in current cycle; check CBRE/Green Street for latest]
- Class B / Secondary markets: 8.00%–10.00% [VERIFY — likely range; significant pricing distress in many submarkets]
- Class C / Tertiary markets: 9.00%–12.00%+ [VERIFY — many assets currently un-financeable; cap rate may not be the right metric]

### Typical Annual Rent Growth Assumptions (defensible underwriting)
- Conservative: 0.0%–1.5% [VERIFY]
- Market: 1.5%–2.5% [VERIFY]
- Aggressive — flag for stress test: greater than 3.0% sustained [VERIFY]

### Typical Operating Expense Ratios (% of EGI)
- Conservative: 35%–40% [VERIFY]
- Market: 40%–50% [VERIFY — varies materially by lease structure (gross vs. NNN) and submarket]

### Typical Hold Periods by Strategy
- Core: 7–10+ years
- Value-Add: 4–7 years (longer than other asset classes due to lease-up timelines)
- Opportunistic: 4–7 years

### Common Red-Flag Thresholds for This Asset Class
- "Lease Rollover Risk Ignored" (KF-02 #6) — when more than 30% of rentable area rolls within the hold period, mark-to-market risk dominates the underwriting.
- "Aggressive Lease-Up Pace" (KF-02 #3) — Class B suburban office lease-up assumptions inside 24 months are rarely supportable in current market conditions.
- "Cap Rate Without Source" (KF-02 #14) — office trade volume is depressed; comp sets are often thin and require unusually careful sourcing.
- "Cycle-Blind Comp Selection" (KF-02 #15) — pre-2020 office trades reflect a different demand environment and should not anchor exit pricing.
- "Concessions Treated as Permanent Rent" (KF-02 #4) — current office concessions (TI packages, free rent of 1+ months per year of lease term) are persistent and should not be assumed to burn off.

## Industrial

### Typical Going-In Cap Rate Ranges (institutional quality)
- Class A / Primary markets: 4.75%–5.75% [VERIFY]
- Class B / Secondary markets: 5.75%–7.00% [VERIFY]
- Class C / Tertiary markets: 7.00%–8.50% [VERIFY]

### Typical Annual Rent Growth Assumptions (defensible underwriting)
- Conservative: 2.5%–3.0% [VERIFY]
- Market: 3.0%–4.5% [VERIFY — varies materially by submarket; coastal infill remains tighter than inland markets]
- Aggressive — flag for stress test: greater than 5.0% sustained [VERIFY — was defensible in 2021–2022; less defensible now]

### Typical Operating Expense Ratios (% of EGI)
- Conservative: 8%–12% [VERIFY — most industrial leases are NNN, so landlord-borne opex is small]
- Market: 12%–18% [VERIFY]

### Typical Hold Periods by Strategy
- Core: 7–10+ years
- Value-Add: 3–5 years
- Opportunistic: 3–5 years (development typically 3–6 years)

### Common Red-Flag Thresholds for This Asset Class
- "Cycle-Blind Comp Selection" (KF-02 #15) — industrial saw extreme rent and cap rate compression in 2021–2022; underwriting that anchors to that period frequently overstates exit pricing.
- "Supply Pipeline Ignored" (KF-02 #22) — many industrial submarkets have material new construction; pipeline-to-inventory ratios above 5% suppress rent growth.
- "Unrealistic Rent Growth" (KF-02 #1) — projections above 5% annual on stabilized Class A require explicit submarket and tenant-demand evidence.
- "Replacement Reserves Understated" (KF-02 #10) — older industrial product (clear height under 28', limited dock doors) requires meaningful capex to remain competitive; model accordingly.
- "Exit at Stabilized Yield, Not Market" (KF-02 #16) — common on build-to-core development memos that conflate yield-on-cost with the buyer's market cap.

## Retail

### Typical Going-In Cap Rate Ranges (institutional quality)
- Class A / Primary markets: 5.75%–7.00% [VERIFY — grocery-anchored and necessity retail typically tighter; power centers wider]
- Class B / Secondary markets: 7.00%–8.50% [VERIFY]
- Class C / Tertiary markets: 8.50%–10.00%+ [VERIFY — wide dispersion; tertiary retail often un-financeable]

### Typical Annual Rent Growth Assumptions (defensible underwriting)
- Conservative: 1.5%–2.0% [VERIFY — most retail leases have fixed escalators of 1–3% baked into in-place leases]
- Market: 2.0%–3.0% [VERIFY]
- Aggressive — flag for stress test: greater than 3.5% sustained [VERIFY]

### Typical Operating Expense Ratios (% of EGI)
- Conservative: 10%–15% [VERIFY — most retail leases are NNN; landlord-borne opex is limited to non-recoverable items]
- Market: 15%–25% [VERIFY — varies with lease structure and recovery percentage]

### Typical Hold Periods by Strategy
- Core: 7–10+ years
- Value-Add: 4–6 years
- Opportunistic: 3–5 years

### Common Red-Flag Thresholds for This Asset Class
- "Lease Rollover Risk Ignored" (KF-02 #6) — anchor and junior anchor expirations within the hold period are existential events for retail centers; co-tenancy clauses can cascade.
- "Aggressive Lease-Up Pace" (KF-02 #3) — backfilling vacant anchor or big-box space frequently takes 18–36+ months and may require capital contributions to the new tenant.
- "Cap Rate Without Source" (KF-02 #14) — retail cap rates vary materially by tenant credit, lease term, and grocery anchor presence; generic "market" claims without specific comps are weak.
- "Hidden Mezz or Preferred Equity" (KF-02 #20) — retail capital stacks frequently include pref equity tranches that materially affect LP economics.
- "Job Growth and Migration Unaddressed" (KF-02 #23) — retail demand is downstream of household formation and disposable income trends in the trade area.

## Hospitality

### Typical Going-In Cap Rate Ranges (institutional quality)
- Class A / Primary markets: 6.50%–8.00% [VERIFY — luxury and full-service typically tighter; select-service wider]
- Class B / Secondary markets: 8.00%–9.50% [VERIFY]
- Class C / Tertiary markets: 9.50%–11.00%+ [VERIFY]

### Typical Annual Rent Growth Assumptions (defensible underwriting)
- Conservative: 2.0%–3.0% RevPAR growth [VERIFY]
- Market: 3.0%–4.5% RevPAR growth [VERIFY — note hospitality underwrites RevPAR (rate × occupancy), not contract rent]
- Aggressive — flag for stress test: greater than 5.0% sustained RevPAR growth [VERIFY]

### Typical Operating Expense Ratios (% of EGI)
- Conservative: 60%–65% [VERIFY — hospitality is operationally intensive; opex ratios are far higher than other asset classes]
- Market: 65%–72% [VERIFY — varies by service level (limited, select, full, luxury)]

### Typical Hold Periods by Strategy
- Core: 5–10 years (shorter than other asset classes due to capex intensity)
- Value-Add: 4–6 years (typically tied to PIP cycles)
- Opportunistic: 3–5 years

### Common Red-Flag Thresholds for This Asset Class
- "Unrealistic Rent Growth" (KF-02 #1) — RevPAR projections above the local STR forecast for the market warrant scrutiny; STR data is the industry standard.
- "Replacement Reserves Understated" (KF-02 #10) — hospitality FF&E reserves typically run 4%–5% of total revenue [VERIFY]; anything below 3% is a flag.
- "Timing Requires Perfect Execution" (KF-02 #24) — PIP renovation timing risk is acute; brand-mandated PIPs have hard deadlines and operational disruption.
- "Management Fee Below Market" (KF-02 #11) — hospitality management and franchise fees combined typically run 8%–12% of revenue [VERIFY]; understated fee structures are common.
- "Exit Cap Compression" (KF-02 #12) — hospitality cap rate compression assumptions are particularly fragile because RevPAR and cap rates both move with the cycle, compounding downside risk.
